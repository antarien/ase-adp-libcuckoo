# (nf-fa-gavel) AOW ADG Project Agreements

**Version AOW-ADG-PV-16/07/01** -- for Collaboration on the Antares Open World (AOW) Work

---

## (nf-fa-book) Preamble & Definitions

These Project Agreements were written with the intent to regulate submitted contributions for the potential use in implementing the AOW concept. The supreme and declared goal of the Project Agreements shall be fidelity to the AOW concept and the implementation of its philosophical guidelines. The AOW concept itself is defined through its continuously developing concept book as a referendum and is situated through the AOW Manuscript.

> [!INFO]
> The AOW ADG Project Agreements form the legal foundation for the collaboration of all members of the Antares Developer Group. They secure the integrity of the AOW concept and regulate the use of submitted contributions.

"We see Antares Open World as a sandbox, also in the sense of 'playing the game with complexity.' We love complexity and metagaming. It is one of the fundamental Antares philosophies to promote the complexity-loving player over the simplicity-loving player. There are enough games on the market that have lost complexity over time through generalization, and have therefore become uninteresting for the creators and founders of Antares Open World."

Become part of the project if you love to immerse yourself in complexity! If you are not deterred by new ideas and unconventional paths. If you love to capture the quintessence of optimality through interaction and complexity as close to reality as possible.

Through the creation of Antares Open World, we wish to establish a new form of gaming culture. In particular, we want Antares Open World to develop an active metagaming-initiated and vibrant player culture, thereby creating a far-reaching and dynamic life of its own.

On this basis, the concept author and founder of the project, Jan Ohlmann, wrote these AOW ADG Project Agreements and brought to life games and projects founded on the concept for Antares Open World. The concept author hereby provides the AOW Manuscript, and thus his implied intellectual property, for non-commercial purposes to the AOW developer community, for the establishment of a novel implementation policy at the community level, respectively the attempt to involve the AOW community in development. The specified usage rights of the concept author apply for the use of the AOW Manuscript.

These Project Agreements, also provisions and/or agreements --including later amendments--, now concern the purpose of securing the integrity, availability, and confidentiality of the aforementioned AOW philosophy and the preservation of concept fidelity during the development and implementation of the initiated project, hereinafter referred to as Antares Open World or AOW, or in connection with the agreements also AOW ADG Project Agreements (AOW-ADG-PV).

The Antares Developer Group, also Antares Dev Group (ADG), or Antares Developer Group, describes itself for the purpose of primary team formation pursuant to Section 1, as well as the evaluation and potential implementation of the project pursuant to this preamble and the AOW Developer License, in the form of a community of interest with shared paths and goals. The ADG thereby undertakes to establish itself as a situated unit, taking into account Section 10 pursuant to the AOW Developer License, as a structural component of this community of interest.

The ADG thus describes itself internally through its active members (contributors and/or collaborators) on the projects (core competencies) of the Antares Open World work. Members of the ADG may, as license holders pursuant to the AOW Developer License, equally act in the role of licensee and/or licensor. The agreements are accepted and concluded by mutual consent of all ADG members. They mutually assure each other of their will to implement.

In passages hereinafter referred to as copyright, all copyright-related rights are included that apply to other types of works. Examples include 3D models, 2D concepts, source code, designs (assets, models, graphics, photos, media), and/or other property of a developer and/or member of the Antares Dev Group (ADG).

A "contribution" refers to all copyrightable works placed under the AOW Developer License. A "goal-oriented contribution" is a contribution with the special characteristic of demonstrated and assessed concept fidelity, as well as the previously announced necessity in the form of an implementation request.

Each ADG member shall be addressed formally and by name in accordance with the member registry (ADG-MGV) maintained by the concept author, as well as by their position within the Antares Dev Group and corresponding responsibilities, on a project-related basis.

Contributions pursuant to Section 1 may not be transferred to other, external projects or successor projects without the consent of the concept author. Successor projects are defined here as all projects composed of parts from former ADG members that do not continue the AOW work as such in its parts.

Excluded are the system boundaries of core competencies as independent AOW projects, prototypes, associated web applications, smartphone apps, as well as later possible successors with increasing version numbers that are created in collaboration with the concept author.

Name changes and the use of working titles remain reserved to the concept author. Otherwise, the provisions and terminology of the AOW Developer License apply.

For this purpose, the following Project Agreements are concluded between the concept author and the ADG member.

## (nf-fa-money) 1 -- Monetization, Kickstarter & Team Formation

### Para 1. Crowd Funding & Financing

Part of the prerequisite for a successful implementation policy of the AOW concept is the exploration and establishment of, but not exclusively, crowd funding mechanisms, such as the Kickstarter platform or proprietary web-based shop systems. To this end, ADG members work toward this goal in their development processes, among other things. The openness to other funding mechanisms and/or financing models shall be preserved.

### Para 2. Netiquette & Formation of the AOW Core Team

The formation of the core team shall become an essential component of the ADG.

Through the organizational unit of the ADG, taking into account enthusiasm, commitment, and teamwork ability, an interest-oriented recommendation should be possible toward all ADG members.

A recommendation shall be assessed according to the criteria of performance, goal-setting, dynamics, structure, and climate. The following framework criteria and netiquette shall guide the goal of good core team formation and help to evaluate it.

> [!TIP]
> The following paragraphs describe the core values of the ADG team culture: synergy, shared goals, and mutual support as the foundation of successful collaboration.

The touchstone of our ADG team is its capability. Our team is able to achieve results that no single ADG member could ever accomplish alone. Personal strengths unite in the ADG team and create our product, which will represent more than the sum of individual talents.

The ADG team commits to the main goal pursuant to the preamble, which all ADG members know in detail, agree with, can identify with, and find worthy of pursuit.

We call this core goal of our team our mission. In addition, there are further personal goals to be achieved. Among other things, every ADG member has a special interest in contributing within the ADG. Worth mentioning here are learning and deepening new skills.

The direction and strength of our ADG core team depend on the agreement of ADG members regarding the main goal and their willingness to place their personal goals in service of the main goal, to commit themselves.

Our ADG team members spur each other on. In community, they feel more comfortable, and they notice that collaborative work continually revitalizes their strength and joy. To describe this unique energy potential of a group, we coin the word synergy.

Through quality and capability, our ADG team is more than the sum of its ADG members. We possess the capacity for synergy, a collective dynamic that can be purposefully built and utilized.

Our ADG team has intelligently resolved the tricky problems of control, leadership claims, work style, organization, and role understanding. The structure of our team is precisely tailored to the tasks to be solved. Individual abilities and subtasks are meaningfully coordinated without many words.

ADG members with leadership aspirations have learned to show consideration for one another and to banish hostilities, competitive thinking, and aggression from team life. Our team manages to work flexibly, empathetically, methodically, and purposefully.

Our ADG team develops a special spirit. It creates openness among all ADG members and mutual joy and encouragement in the project. All ADG members identify with the entire team; success or failure affects their mood, and they will seek with full force to protect the interests of our ADG team. In our team, there is a climate in which all ADG members build trust in one another, openly discuss personal difficulties, and are willing to take risks.

## (nf-fa-balance_scale) 2 -- Rights & Obligations of an ADG Member

The ADG member is obligated, even before collaboration begins, to provide all necessary information (full name, date of birth, place of residence, etc.) that concerns future cooperation and thus the provision of contributions regarding their intended use and/or purpose, completely and truthfully. The member registry serves as reference.

The ADG member is aware that deliberate misinformation may lead to exclusion from the AOW project. The concept author, pursuant to the preamble, is entitled to obtain additional information and, if necessary, to independently research and store personal data in advance, within the framework of data protection law and while safeguarding personal rights. In case of uncertain information, the ADG member must expressly indicate any gaps or potential uncertainties.

> [!WARNING]
> Acceptance of the Project Agreements obligates compliance with all principles and netiquettes. Violations may lead to exclusion from the AOW project. Additionally, confidentiality obligations and careful handling of transferred materials are mandatory.

By accepting these Project Agreements, the ADG member commits to adhering to the concept, the published principles and netiquettes in the form of contribution guidelines. Violations of these principles and netiquettes may lead to exclusion from the AOW project.

The ADG member is obligated to handle any issued software or other third-party proprietary assets, which were provided in the course of contribution to the AOW project, with care and to refrain from misuse and/or transfer to third parties, and also to take appropriate precautions within their means to prevent this. In case of loss of transferred contributions, the ADG member commits to immediately inform about the loss.

The ADG member commits to only storing, managing, transmitting, and making accessible on the provided storage (e.g., version control system) such content that does not violate applicable law or the rights of third parties, nor the provisions of this license agreement, nor could be considered morally reprehensible.

The ADG member commits to indemnifying the AOW project and other ADG members from all claims by third parties arising from the contributions and/or behavior of the ADG member.

The ADG member commits to keeping contributions up to date redundantly on the provided storage locations (servers, collaboration systems, platforms, etc.).

The ADG member commits to treating internal and strategic arrangements classified as confidential by the project committee as confidential.

The ADG member commits to maintaining clean and well-documented work practices. In particular, source code must be documented beyond the normal level of transparency to ensure appropriate integrity and collaboration. In this context, the ADG member commits to transferring all implementations, at the latest upon creation of the source code or upload to version control (GitHub and/or Bitbucket), into our Enterprise Architect solution by Sparx Systems, in the form of use cases and class diagrams through prior UML modeling, or at the latest through reverse engineering into the work packages and software modules provided to them.

The ADG member commits to updating changes and/or progress in their work area to the respective Pivotal Tracker projects.

## (nf-fa-sign_out) 4 -- Departure of an ADG Member

These Project Agreements define both the temporary and permanent departure from the Antares Dev Group. Should corresponding situations (loss of contact, inactivity, exclusion) from paragraphs 1-3 occur, the AOW Developer License Agreement, as part of these agreements, is considered not revoked. The permissions under Section 2 of the AOW Developer License remain fully in effect.

### Para 1. Departure Through Inactivity

After a period of one calendar month of inactivity, without prior declaration of absence, the member automatically transitions to the role of inactive member, pursuant to the definitions in the preamble. Should the declaration of absence define more than 2 calendar months, the member is considered inactive from the date of declaration.

Furthermore, all ADG members who have not yet made a contribution since joining, or who have not made a goal-oriented contribution to the project's continuation for more than one month, are considered inactive members. This includes, in particular, the typical tasks such as goal-oriented contributions arising from the member's assigned role. An unexpected break in communication counts as grounds for deactivation of the member.

After the expiration of 3 calendar months of inactivity and/or loss of contact with an ADG member, the member is considered temporarily departed.

> [!NOTE]
> Inactivity deadlines are staggered: 1 month without explanation leads to "inactive" status, 3 months to "temporarily departed" status. A prior declaration of absence can influence these deadlines.

### Para 2. Death or Other Extraordinary Circumstances

Should an ADG member unexpectedly pass away or otherwise be unable to exercise their rights, the corresponding revocation rights transfer to surviving third parties or a potential legal guardian. In this case as well, the guarantees allocated under Section 2 of the AOW Developer License apply. Corresponding exercise of these rights may only occur after legitimation (certificate of inheritance, guardianship, etc.). A deadline of 2 months is set here.

### Para 3. Departure from the Team Formation Process

Should there be legitimate doubts about the authenticity of the ADG member, and/or compromising disruptions to team formation occur, the concept author is entitled to exclude the ADG member from the project at any time without entitlement to compensation claims. A prior warning may be issued by the concept author. The ADG member is then considered permanently departed. The preservation of their rights under copyright law, as well as all granted guarantees under Section 2 of the AOW Developer License, remain unaffected.

### Para 4. Re-entry

Re-entry is generally possible for temporarily departed members; however, it requires renewed acceptance of the currently valid AOW Developer License and its AOW Project Agreements, as well as the consent of the concept author.

Re-entry for permanently departed members is not provided for.

## (nf-fa-users) 5 -- Advisory Board

The individual AOW projects, organized by core competencies, are managed through an AOW Advisory Board. Additionally, each project group, which finds its designation in the nomenclature ADG/N, includes a project leader. The leadership may, however, be assumed in the form of a role spanning multiple AOW project groups.

The Advisory Board is chaired by the concept author, who is responsible for maintaining and updating the member registry, managing externally obtained licenses, granting exploitation rights in external and/or internal relations, and authorizing submitted patents and utility models.

Furthermore, the Advisory Board functions as an arbitration body in case of disputes; corresponding decisions must be followed. The Advisory Board is obligated to judge objectively and in the best interest of the project in such cases.

The concept author may also delegate additional tasks to the Advisory Board, including primarily, but not exclusively, the management of financial aspects. Additional tasks are to be documented in writing.

The Advisory Board is a dynamic body. As chairperson, the concept author is responsible for classifying and evaluating contributions as goal-oriented and concept-faithful, as well as making goal-oriented changes to the structure pursuant to Section 1 Team Formation & Netiquette. Furthermore, they may appoint and/or dismiss members to the Advisory Board without stating reasons. The size of the Advisory Board may vary depending on the size of the ADG.

A new election of the chairperson occurs upon death by the existing members of the Advisory Board. The interests of the concept author are to be preserved and continued in their memory.

The concept author decides on the granting and/or withdrawal of additional positions on the board, and/or the admission of new ADG members.

## (nf-fa-calendar) 6 -- Entry into Force & Changes to the Agreements

The copyright holder (concept author), pursuant to the preamble of this license, may at any time publish revised and/or new versions of this license. Such new versions may correspond to the current license but may also deviate entirely to address new problems and requirements.

Each version of these Project Agreements shall have a new and unique version number with the date of its entry into force.

The existing and submitted contributions are automatically transferred to the later version "any later version" on the date of its entry into force.

The provisions of the named current version must be followed, or those of any later version published by the author of the AOW ADG Project Agreement.

If the contribution does not specify a version number, the current version published by the author of the AOW Agreement automatically applies.

> [!WARNING]
> No objection within 14 calendar days of publication means full acceptance of the new version. This also applies retroactively to contributions already made. Objections apply only to the explicitly stated passage.

Should no objection be raised within 14 calendar days of publication and thus entry into force of the AOW ADG Agreement --also applicable to future amendments to the agreement-- by existing active ADG members, this license and/or its amendments shall apply in full from the date of publication. This also applies to contributions already made to the project pursuant to Section 1, Para 4 of the AOW Developer License.

Should an active member raise an objection to the newly established agreement, the objection applies exclusively to the passage formulated in the objection. The existing agreement retains its validity. It is then to be weighed, for the benefit of the concept, to what extent the conflict of interest of the individual is reconcilable with the conformity of the new amended passage of this agreement.
